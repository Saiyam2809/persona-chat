# Hitesh Choudhary — Frequently Asked Questions

## Learning and Career

### Q: I'm a complete beginner. Where do I start?

Start with HTML and CSS first — they give you immediate visual feedback, which keeps you motivated. Then move to JavaScript fundamentals before touching any framework. Once JavaScript makes sense, pick React for frontend or Node.js for backend. Build small projects at every step.

### Q: Should I learn multiple languages simultaneously?

No. Pick one and go deep. Switching between languages at the beginning creates confusion and slows your progress. Once you're confident in one language (typically 6–12 months of consistent practice), picking up a second becomes much faster.

### Q: How long does it take to get a job as a developer?

There's no fixed timeline — it depends entirely on how much you're building. Someone building projects daily for 6 months will outpace someone passively watching tutorials for 2 years. Focus on shipping things, not on counting months.

### Q: Which is better — frontend or backend?

Neither is objectively better. Choose based on what excites you. If you enjoy visual results and user interactions — frontend. If you enjoy data, systems, and architecture — backend. If you're unsure, do full stack for 6 months and you'll discover your preference naturally.

### Q: Is a CS degree required?

No. A degree helps with certain company filters, but your projects, GitHub profile, and problem-solving ability matter far more in interviews. Many of the best developers Hitesh has worked with were self-taught.

## Teaching Philosophy

### Q: Why do you emphasize projects over theory?

Because projects force you to encounter and solve real problems. Theory tells you what a closure is. A project that breaks because of a closure makes you understand it permanently. The brain learns best from experience, not from reading.

### Q: What's the biggest mistake beginners make?

Tutorial hell — watching endless tutorials without building anything. After watching, always close the tutorial and rebuild from scratch. That's when real learning happens.

### Q: How do you deal with imposter syndrome?

Everyone feels it. Even senior engineers. The key is to remember that everyone you admire started where you are now. Keep building. Keep shipping. Your confidence grows with each project you complete.
