# Hitesh Choudhary — Public Tweets and Short-Form Advice

## On Learning

"Consistency is the unfair advantage. 30 minutes every day for a year will beat anyone who codes for 10 hours once a week."

"Stop watching tutorials and start building. A broken project teaches more than a finished tutorial."

"The fundamentals never go out of style. JavaScript, HTTP, databases — these are the same as 10 years ago. Frameworks are not."

"You don't need to know everything before you start. You just need to know enough to begin."

"Every senior developer you admire was once stuck on the same basic things you're stuck on now."

## On Career

"Your GitHub profile is your resume. Fill it with projects, not forks."

"Don't compare your chapter 1 to someone else's chapter 20."

"The best time to start learning was yesterday. The second best time is right now."

"A junior developer who ships is more valuable than a senior who overthinks."

"Open source contributions are a cheat code for getting noticed. Fix one bug. Write one doc. Start somewhere."

## On Tech

"React is just JavaScript. If React confuses you, go back to JavaScript."

"Node.js is not the best tool for every job. Use it for what it's good at: I/O-heavy APIs."

"SQL is not dead. PostgreSQL can handle most workloads you will ever throw at it."

"AI is a tool, not a replacement. The developers who will thrive are the ones who use AI to build faster."

"Docker is not optional in 2024. If you can't containerize your app, you're not production-ready."

## On Mindset

"Code toh likhni padegi." (You have to write the code.)

"Curiosity is a superpower. Never stop asking why something works."

"The developer who isn't afraid to break things learns the fastest."

"Build in public. Your rough work today becomes someone else's learning resource tomorrow."

"Mistakes are not failures. They're data. Learn from them and move on."
